Problems Identified

Sense of Claustrophobia During the pandemic, students are advised to stay at home for safety concerns. Consequently, some students may have developed slight claustrophobia by feeling trapped in their home, and not being able to go out for a walk.
Boredom Spending their whole day at home, students have a very limited choice of activities other than going to online lessons. As a result, this may cause students to struggle from boredom, which may further develop into depression, anxiety, poor social skills and a lot more unforeseeable consequences.
Loneliness Staying at home for most of the time, students are socially isolated from their friends and extended family. Sometimes, students are also left at home alone because of their parent’s work. This often causes students to feel lonely when they are alone at home, which may cause increased stress, and antisocial behavior.
Family Relationship Being with your parents all the time might cause disagreements on a lot of issues.

Claustrophobia

With the pandemic restrictions, the chances to meet up with friends are no doubt decreased or even none. for example in Hong Kong, you can only go into the streets in a group of 2 at most, which means you can only go out with one friend at most. Though these policies are built or set to protect the spread of the virus, it affects students on their social life, it distances their relationships with your best friends or even shrinks your social circle.

So what can you do to at least improve the situation?

  • If you all love to play video games, make an appointment with your best friends and game online together, using apps like Discord, you can enjoy the moment, release stress and bond with your friends during games.
  • If you don’t enjoy gaming, have group calls, facetime, zoom, chat with one another during spare time. You can also play many of the online party games with them. This will avoid loneliness and gives you access to communicate with the friends you miss!

Boredom

Each family has their own habit of meeting with extended families, some will meet once a week, some meets once in a month or even just in holidays and festivals. People all around the world, whether they are from an Eastern or Western culture, meeting with extended families is always seen as important. But with the pandemic, neither of them can meet up based on their habits and traditions.

So how does the pandemic affect them? Take Chinese as an example, the Chinese new year is coming up, and as tradition, different nuclear family would meet up with their extended families, people would traditionally have a meal together, exchange red pockets and simply celebrate. However, this year, the pandemic bans different families from meeting and having a meal together as it will increase the risk of infection. On the other hand, western families tend to enjoy meeting and gathering in weekends for all short of activities, BBQ in the backyard, Super Bowl parties, American Thanksgiving… They tend to choose to enjoy life with friends and families, but again, with the pandemic striking the whole world, these activities were banned or decreased.

How do I safely meet up with family?

In order to solve the boredom and social distancing problems, try to hold online gatherings! If you insists to gather in person, be sure that it is not a large gathering and you have sufficient amount of hygiene resources like masks, hand sanitizers etc. and be sure to keep your masks on and social distancing of at least one meter. For more guidance: CDC gathering advice.

Loneliness

As society develops, more and more people feel distanced from others. However, "Humans are wired to come together physically," says psychologist Judith Moskowitz of Northwestern University. The lockdowns imposed with the pandemic made the issue of loneliness far worse. Students are forced to study online, essentially taking away the one thing that most people find enjoyable about school—being able to meet friends.

Luckily, we have some solutions if you’re stuck at home and feeling a little bit down because you are alone.

  • Art can be a good way to express yourself and get other people’s attention. It can be very powerful for centring and positioning — a way to explore your own identity and meet new people. You can make fan art, post edits, and gather a circle of friends with your own interest to keep yourself entertained.
  • Keeping friendships alive with small acts of kindness. Even something simple and small can help you maintain a friendship and make yourself feel fulfilled. By helping someone, talking to them when they’re down, or even just sharing a joke, all keep your friendship afloat.

Family relationship

As teenagers, dealing with your parents is already infuriating normally. With the lockdown being imposed, it is even harder to deal with the tensions that may build up between you and your parents.

Here are some ways you can possibly minimize family conflict:

  • As a teenager, you have the right to demand some time alone. Most parents will likely understand that students, especially when they have a lot of school work, need a significant amount of time to themselves.
  • Ask your parents about what makes them happy. As long as you do not cross their defined bottom line, you can avoid many long and heated arguments.
