Meeting up with friends

With the pandemic restrictions, the chances to meet up with friends are no doubt decreased or even none. for example in Hong Kong, you can only go into the streets in a group of 2 at most, which means you can only go out with one friend at most. Though these policies are built or set to protect the spread of the virus, it affects students on their social life, it distances their relationships with your best friends or even shrinks your social circle.

So what can you do to at least improve the situation?

  • If you all love to play video games, make an appointment with your best friends and game online together, using apps like Discord, you can enjoy the moment, release stress and bond with your friends during games.
  • If you donâ€™t enjoy gaming, have group calls, facetime, zoom, chat with one another during spare time. You can also play many of the online party games with them. This will avoid loneliness and gives you access to communicate with the friends you miss!

Meeting up with extended family

Each family has their own habit of meeting with extended families, some will meet once a week, some meets once in a month or even just in holidays and festivals. People all around the world, whether they are from an Eastern or Western culture, meeting with extended families is always seen as important. But with the pandemic, neither of them can meet up based on their habits and traditions.

So how does the pandemic affect them? Take Chinese as an example, the Chinese new year is coming up, and as tradition, different nuclear family would meet up with their extended families, people would traditionally have a meal together, exchange red pockets and simply celebrate. However, this year, the pandemic bans different families from meeting and having a meal together as it will increase the risk of infection. On the other hand, western families tend to enjoy meeting and gathering in weekends for all short of activities, BBQ in the backyard, Super Bowl parties, American Thanksgivingâ€¦ They tend to choose to enjoy life with friends and families, but again, with the pandemic striking the whole world, these activities were banned or decreased.

How do I safely meet up with family?

In order to solve the boredom and social distancing problems, try to hold online gatherings! If you insists to gather in person, be sure that it is not a large gathering and you have sufficient amount of hygiene resources like masks, hand sanitizers etc. and be sure to keep your masks on and social distancing of at least one meter. For more guidance: CDC gathering advice.

Cybersecurity

Without many activities to take part in during the pandemic, most students tend to spend their time on social media. However, most students are not aware of the different dangers they may face online. For example, there are many incidents related to the scamming of money or stolen personal data.

How do I protect myself from online threats?

  • At no point should you frivolously give personal information without second consideration.
  • When you decide to visit websites or download apps or was even asked to fill in your personal information for certain benefits.
  • Be sure to download apps to keep check of your cybersecurity. Personally, we recommend Malwarebytes for Windows and CleanMyMacX for Mac because we found that they are reliable.
  • Additionaslly, installing restrictions to certain unsafe websites is also a good idea.
    • If you would like to know more about how to identify and respond to cyber threats we recommend this webpage from the European Union.

However, you have to be mentally prepared that this relationship can end in seconds.
